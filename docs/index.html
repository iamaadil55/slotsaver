What makes it trustworthy

Built the way medical decision-support should be built.

Honest probabilities

Isotonic-calibrated: when SlotSaver says 40%, about 40% actually miss. Calibration error measured at 0.015 on held-out future weeks.

Reasons, not black boxes

Every flag explains itself — "booked 32 days ahead · 2 prior no-shows" — so staff can trust and verify each call.

Fairness-audited

Per-group calibration and benefit rates are checked on every retrain. Risk scores trigger help — reminders, rescheduling, transport info — never denial of care.

Drift-monitored

Booking patterns change. SlotSaver watches its own inputs (PSI) and flags itself for retraining before quality decays.

Capacity-aware

Your staff can make 20 calls a day, not 500. SlotSaver ranks per patient — not per appointment — and respects real economics.

Runs on your data

No EHR integration required to start: an appointment spreadsheet is enough to train a clinic-specific model.

How a pilot works

Share appointment history. An Excel/CSV export of past appointments (no names needed — dates, demographics, attendance).
SlotSaver trains and validates on your clinic. Time-based testing only: trained on your past, judged on your recent weeks.
Front desk gets a daily call list. Ranked, capped to your capacity, with reasons — via dashboard today, inside your workflow tomorrow.
We measure honestly. No-show rate before vs after, reported with the same rigor as the model itself.
Where this project honestly stands: SlotSaver is a working research prototype — model, calibration, explanations, API, monitoring, and fairness auditing are built and tested on public data (all code and results are open). It has not yet run a clinical pilot, and no causal claim is made about intervention effect until a randomized pilot measures it. If you run a clinic and want to be the first pilot, that's exactly the conversation we're looking for.
